I had been fantacising about this trip for a good few years - a Gujarat Rajasthan trip, in my own vehicle, free of any worry in the world. Everything aligned in Dec 2023 and a happier chubbier me got to thoroughly experience this.

Lasted 17 days, 5500 km, starting from Bangalore, reaching Pakistan border, and back to Bangalore. In my beloved Seltos. What an incredible mile muncher.

This post is very detailed, with like ~400 photos. Take your time, savour it over several days. In part, this was written for future me so that I could reminisce my days of adventure.

<Stop title="Roaming around Pune" coord={[73.7922234, 18.5037251]}>

Spent the next day roaming around Pune with a friend (my junior from prev workplace), who hosted me at his place for some authentic breakfast, and showed me around. There was upma, "Nachni" - made from Ragi, and chilly oil. Did I mention they REALLY like spicy food? We then headed to a local poha place - which you guessed it, comes with spicy water (very tasty tho, no complaints) and roamed around a bit.

Pune feels very Marathi. I'd say it's like the Mysore of Maharashtra, the cultural capital if you will. Shivaji Maharaj is well revered and the food and culture is unique. Doesn't feel like a greneric Indian city.

<Stop title="Towards Gujarat" coord={[70.6448386, 22.2990165]}>

Pune -> skip Mumbai -> Kathiyawadi

An interesting fact I learned during this trip was that there were very many good hotels in the middle of nowhere along the highway. I often thought I had to make pit stops at larger cities, but that wasn't necessary. There was this really cool hotel somewhere between Mumbai and Gujarat border that offered a nice clean small room, and good food. Because of this, I could skip all of Mumbai (which I had been to a couple years ago). Also the Pune -> Mumbai highway is super good (with very many cameras) and a nice food stop.
The roads started to get really crappy again, and oh god the amount of trucks will kill you. Once you enter Gujarat, the roads get super good agai, but it's alll truck traffic. Very boring.

There's a very interesting temple on the way to Jodhpur with a super interesting diety - yes, a motorcycle!

The story is that the rider Om Singh Rathore happened to have crashed and died at the location of this temple, following which the police impounded this bike and took it to the police station. However, the next morning the bike magically had re-appeared at the crash site and the police had to drain the fuel and take it back again along with locking it up. And yet again the bike had mysteriously re-appeared at the same location, following a cycle of locking it up and it re-appearing it again - the locals had then believed that this bike had supernatural powers.
I was told that this information was logged in the police records.
